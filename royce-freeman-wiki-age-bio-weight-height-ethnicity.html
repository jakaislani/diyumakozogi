    How did Royce Freeman start his Professional Career?

    Freeman came to Oregon as the top-rated recruit in the 2014 class. However, he impressed coaches during practice and camp, competing for the starting job with Byron Marshall and Thomas Tyner. To start the 2014 season, all three were considered co-starters at running back.

    Freeman, the largest of the three, established himself as a “power back,” which the Oregon offense has lacked since the 2008 season. He finished the season with 252 carries, 1,365 yards, and 18 touchdowns, as well as 16 receptions for 158 yards and one score.

    He also delivered a 26-yard touchdown pass to quarterback Marcus Mariota in a 31-24 loss to the Arizona Wildcats. Following the season, he was named the Pac-12 Conference Offensive Freshman of the Year. Freeman started the 2015 season strong, rushing for 180 yards and three touchdowns against Eastern Washington.

    On October 3, he carried for 163 yards and two touchdowns in a 41-24 victory over Colorado. Moreover, the next week, against Washington State, he recorded 246 running yards, two rushing touchdowns, and a receiving score in a 45-38 double-overtime defeat. The following week, he rushed for 138 yards in a win over Washington.

In the Alamo Bowl against TCU, he ran for 130 yards and three touchdowns in a 47-41 three-overtime loss.

    As a sophomore, Freeman rushed for 1,836 yards and 17 touchdowns, while also catching 26 receptions for 348 yards and two touchdowns. He led the Pac-12 in rushing touchdowns this season.
